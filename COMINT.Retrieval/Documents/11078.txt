an indexing system' switching language for integrating
    automated information networks. (phd thesis-case western
    reserve university, ohio.) an account of the basic concepts,
    problem areas, and operational characteristics and techniques
    of switching languages, concentrating particularly on the
    design and development of classified concept coordinate
    indexing as providing the necessary switching mechanism for
    information transfer in a network. it is based on a synthesis
    of pre- and post-coordinate indexing and enables free
    coordination of diverse concepts, through keyword inverted
    indexing based on an 2a priori1 set of classes.

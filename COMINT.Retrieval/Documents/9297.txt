facsimile transmits typed/signed messages at low-cost.
    both analog and digital facsimile transmission have advantages,
    depending on user needs. both have electronic mail capabilities
    and can be used in conjunction with a 'value-added network' to
    communicate with otherwise-incompatible equipment. criteria to
    be considered when selecting facsimile equipment are maximum
    document size accepted; operating speed; copy quality;
    purchase, rental, and running costs; and equipment size.

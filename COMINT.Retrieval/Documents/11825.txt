the world turned upside down' reference priorities and
    the state archives. examines the problems associated with the
    heavy demands made on state archives by genealogists. draws
    upon the experience of the maryland hall of records where, in
    1980, 75% of staff time was spent answering almost 8000
    genealogical enquiries. describes some of the changes which
    were made in order to cope more effectively with this
    demand.

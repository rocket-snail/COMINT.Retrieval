national bibliographies of the finno-ugrian peoples in
    the soviet union. there are 4.5 million ugrian finns in the
    ussr in 6 different autonomous republics, each with its own
    language, some using roman and others cyrillic alphabets.
    publishing is very local and only newspapers in these minority
    languages are occasionally available outside the area of
    origin. books are listed in the 2soviet national bibliography1,
    but only in the russian language. each autonomous republic
    issues its own national bibliography, the oldest established
    being the estonian which began in 1946 and appears quarterly.
    describes the format and scope of these local bibliographies in
    some detail.

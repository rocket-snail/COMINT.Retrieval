the mechanics of stock-taking in libraries: a case study
    of indian institute of technology, delhi. examines the problem
    of stock-taking of books in indian libraries; describes
    stock-taking methods used in india, their strong points, and
    their limitations; and discusses the desirability of
    stock-taking. suggests that in large libraries only
    computer-based stock-taking can succeed. describes such an
    operation in the indian institute of technology, delhi, in 1977
    and provides the cost analysis.

life style research' an aid to promoting public
    libraries. describes a life style research study of 423
    residents aged 25-64 in the 5-county metropolitan area of
    columbus, ohio, commissioned by the great traib broadcasting
    company to gain a better understanding of its radio audience.
    the data, analysed by oclc, suggests an approach to library use
    promotion that expressively demonstrates how library services
    can fit into preexisting needs and interests. the public should
    be shown how library services can help to solve real life
    problems. the life style variables can help pinpoint these
    problems and suggest promotional themes and ideas.

united states secondary information services in physical
    sciences and engineering' evolution and trends from sputnik to
    nixon. (dls thesis-catholic university of america, washington,
    dc.) identifies, analyses, and evaluates major factors that
    influenced the evolution of us secondary information services
    and their producers in physical sciences and engineering,
    1957-71. key personnel at 4 producers (h.w. wilson, engineering
    index, inc., us atomic energy commission, and the institute for
    scientific information) have been interviewed and some of their
    products (2applied science and technology index, engineering
    index, nuclear science abstracts1, and 2science citation
    index1) have been analysed in detail.

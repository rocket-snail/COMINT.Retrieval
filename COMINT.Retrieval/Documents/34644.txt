    the vienna centre. created in 1963, the european co-ordination
    centre for research and documentation in social sciences (known
    as the vienna centre) is an attempt to broaden social science
    perspectives and contribute to the formulation of problems,
    methods, and solutions on a supranational basis. its original
    broad remit (still adhered to) was to 'stimulate, mainly in
    europe, international comparative researchers in social
    sciences'. outlines the centre's past achievements and
    describes the challenging nature of its work.

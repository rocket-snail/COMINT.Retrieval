walter hofmann's institute for the study of readers and
    the written word. the establishment of the institute in leipzig
    came as the climax of the german librarian, walter hofmann's
    career. discusses the development of the institute from its
    inception up to hofmann's retirement. its development faced
    several crisis, both internal and external. the institute's
    achievements include studies of reader behaviour, the book, and
    the library in its research department, and in the department
    for literary reference works they include the leipzig system of
    catalogues, as well as extensive reviewing of books.

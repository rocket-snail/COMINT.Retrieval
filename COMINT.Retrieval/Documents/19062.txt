education for the use of machine readable reference
    retrieval services. the evaluation of machine-readable
    reference retrieval services into a major resource for
    technology transfer has been attended by a host of technical
    communication problems which are being addressed through a
    variety of educational approaches. presents an overview of
    developmental patterns for these emerging reference retrieval
    services and relates them in a 'cause-effect' perspective.
    using a 'word picture', depicts the organisational and
    functional components of the bibliographic chain (information
    generation-transformation-use). presents a variety of
    educational approaches to end user audiences and explores the
    role of the producers of the services.

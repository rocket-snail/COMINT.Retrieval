reflections of a black librarian. the author was
    recruited into the us library profession in the 1950s because
    of an expressed need for more black male librarians. he decided
    to work in black academic institutions because he felt his
    expertise was needed there. the circumstances surrounding such
    establishments compelled him to give quality library service
    with limited financial resources. claims that because of this
    black librarians have to be twice as good. discusses the unique
    role of black librarians in the educational system and the
    hurdles they must conquer.

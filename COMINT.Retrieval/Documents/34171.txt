professional education changes course again. considers
    the desirability of continuing education for librarians, and
    describes the different ways in which uk library schools are
    participating in this process. discusses the provision for, and
    potential of' short courses; part-time 1st degree and
    post-graduate courses in librarianship; taught ma courses in
    library and information studies; and research degrees. also
    considers the possibility of library schools becoming involved,
    through bec, with sub-professional level courses. analyses the
    results of 2 surveys designed to judge the effectiveness of the
    library school contribution to staff development in recent
    years.

energy regulations for libraries, too?. in 1979, the us
    department of energy announced that public hearings on
    regulations for emergency building temperature restrictions
    would be held in 5 principal cities, and in response, various
    major associations consulted at length about the impact of the
    regulations and testified to the department. the department was
    urged to exempt museums, archives, research libraries and
    historic structures where the preservation of cultural and
    scientific heritage requires specific temperature and/or
    relative humidity levels. describes the librarians' attempts to
    conform to the flexible provisions, and their efforts to obtain
    exemptions to the programme.

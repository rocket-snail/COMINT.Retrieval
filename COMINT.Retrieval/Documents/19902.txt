text editiong for librarians' trading the typewriter for
    the terminal. a brief guide to the use of text editors in a
    library situation. a text editor is 1 or more computer programs
    allowing the user to enter, manipulate, store and retrieve text
    with the assistance of a large computer. a potential user
    should investigate the general characteristics of an editor,
    especially the extent to which individual characters can be
    edited, the range of features available for editing proper, and
    the facilities and equipment available for printing, before
    making a commitment to computer-based text editing. outlines
    advantages and disadvantages of the process.

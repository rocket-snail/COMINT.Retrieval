evaluating bibliographic education' a review and
    critique. contribution to a thematic issue on library use
    instruction. discusses some reasons for evaluating
    bibliographic instruction programmes. describes the use of the
    test, the survey, and illuminative evaluation as methods of
    evaluation by various us and european educational institutions.
    considers the statistical and other problems involved with
    these techniques, and argues that none of them are completely
    satisfactory, and that it may be some time before testing
    procedures catch up with user instruction programmes.

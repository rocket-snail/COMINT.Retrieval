unido helps developing nations choose technology. the
    industrial and technological information bank (intib) was
    started on a trial basis by the united nations industrial
    development organization in 1977. it operates in 4 sectors of
    particular importance to developing countries-iron and steel,
    fertilisers, agroindustries, and agricultural machinery.
    achievements include profiles on alternative technologies;
    networks of contacts among information centres and between them
    and users; a preliminary inventory of indigenous technologies;
    a clearinghouse for information relating to contracts; and a
    joint patent programme with the world intellectual property
    organization. cooperative arrangements with other united
    nations agencies and with development finance institutions are
    being planned. information on new energy sources is being
    published.

private videotex systems and access via public services.
    while the initial concept of videotex was that of a home
    information retrieval service, in practice videotex services
    are of much interest to business users. private videotex
    systems are being introduced to complement public services.
    they may be implemented in several different ways-on dedicated
    computers accessed via the public telephone or data networks,
    or via a gateway facility on a public service. examines these
    approaches, and in particular their relative costs.

grant funds provide impetus for comprehensive
    conservation program. research libraries everywhere face the
    problem of conservation of library materials. funding from
    federal government has, in the past few years, stimulated
    research, special projects, and programmes such as the national
    conservation advisory council and the national preservation
    program in the usa. morris library, at the university of
    illinois, has embarked upon a comprehensive conservation
    programme for its collections with grant funds from several
    funding bodies. its programme is described in detail. committed
    to conservation on a broad scale, the university and morris
    library support efforts aimed at preserving and maintaining
    research materials vital to the university's educational and
    research programmes, and the scholarly community at
    large.

commonwealth official publications and the razor gang'
    some thoughts. reviews briefly the cuts in australian federal
    expenditure recently recommended by the review of commonwealth
    functions as they affect the publishing activities of the
    federal executive. discusses the implications of the
    recommended measures which include the leasing of the agps
    bookshops to the private sector, and restricting the
    ministerial document service, and mentions several publications
    which are to cease. urges librarians to carefully monitor the
    effects of these economies in publishing, and to make known to
    official channels their concern about the creation of adequate
    processes of consultation on official publishing.

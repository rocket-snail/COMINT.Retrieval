the involvement of the librarian in the total educational
    process. contribution to a thematic issue on library use
    instruction. examines the extent to which programmes of
    bibliographic instruction have been integrated into the
    educational process of the us college or university. reviews
    the development of library instruction programmes since the
    1930s, and argues that true involvement in the total
    educational planning process is still unrealised on a vast
    scale, and that the classroom instructor prefers to remain
    independent of librarians when structuring academic
    programmes.

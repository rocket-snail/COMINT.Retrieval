the pittsburgh regional library center: a case study on
    the development of a 'full service network'. examines the
    development, operation and future of the pittsburgh regional
    library center (prlc) a multi-state, multi-type library
    network. incorporated in 1967, prlc has developed into a 'full
    service network' which provides oclc service, rlg/rlin service,
    continuing education, on-line union listing, consulting,
    cooperative purchasing and reciprocal borrowing to its 65
    library members in 3 states. discusses prlc's plans for the
    1980s.

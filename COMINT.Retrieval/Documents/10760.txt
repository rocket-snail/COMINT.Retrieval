the cataloging and classification of machine-readable
    data files. part i. a case for incorporating records of
    machine-readable data files into the public catalog. part i of
    a 3-part article. explores the feasibility and desirability of
    incorporating records of machine-readable data files (mrdf) in
    us public library catalogues by considering the existing
    conditions which motivate people to raise the issue; the
    various efforts at solving the problems of documentation and
    bibliographic control of mrdf; the current status of mrdf
    cataloguing activities; and the author's 11 recommendations
    regarding mrdf bibliographic control.

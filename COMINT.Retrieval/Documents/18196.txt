living with ddc 19; not living with ddc 19. presents
    reactions of 2 librarians to reclassifying their libraries
    according to the 19th edition of the dewey decimal
    classification. elizabeth dickinson describes how stockton
    public library (california) began the process of adaptation
    almost as soon as the new edition was published and outlines
    the working principles, public impact and critiques and
    responses with lessons that have been learned. for josephine
    rine at minneapolis community college (minnesota), with only a
    small staff, reclassification is an impossible dream.

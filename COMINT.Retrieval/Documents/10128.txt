monographs in microform' issues in cataloguing and
    bibliographic control. during the last 20 years, microform
    acquisitions have grown at an incredible rate along with
    widespread recognition of inadequate bibliographic access to
    these materials. local bibliographic control over monographs in
    microform has been a particular problem-research libraries have
    failed to provide cataloguing for these mateials with the same
    level of effort and priority accorded to similar materials in
    hard copy. identifies the magnitude of the microform problem,
    reviews the history of attitudes and practices regarding the
    cataloguing of microforms and suggests that integrated
    bibliographic access be implemented at local and national
    levels in the usa.

fragments of a village librarian's diary: 'poison' and
    rolls. describes the life of a part-time librarian in lovel, a
    village with a population of 1000, of which 150 are
    schoolchildren, lying 10 km from the city of viborg, jutland.
    the library, which began in the mid-60s in 1 of the school's
    classrooms, moved in 1979 to a room of its own in the new
    school. the library is open for an hour twice a week. describes
    the close contact with readers and problems involved in
    answering their sometimes unusual requests. services to the
    housebound and pensioners are arranged informally and carried
    out by neighbourly visits. recalls are mainly by word of mouth
    and dates are not strictly adhered to.

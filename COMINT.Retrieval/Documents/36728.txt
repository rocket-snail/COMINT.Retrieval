libraries on the move? scottish rural mobiles. mobile
    library service characteristics and practices differ widely in
    rural scotland with no 1 authority adhering rigidly to any
    standard. where and when a mobile stops are important factors
    in its use. as most visits are on weekdays between 9.00 am and
    5.00 pm, more women than men use the service. most users live
    within 1/4 of a mile of a stopping point. the service is
    greatly valued by users valued by users although it is
    generally inferior to a static branch. nevertheless, urgent
    study is required to identify rural dwellers' needs so that the
    appropriate service can be provided.

leirfjord library' a small community gambles heavily on
    its library. leirfjord, norway, has a scattered population of
    2,200 and small financial resources. in may 80 new library
    premises opened in a former community centre. adult and
    children's departments and the librarian's office occupy 127
    sq.m. on the ground floor, while the 1st floor will house
    study-rooms, stacks and staff facilities. the local authority
    obtained state help towards alterations and equipment, but
    operational costs are high; however, the library is now fully
    active and its use is steadily increasing.

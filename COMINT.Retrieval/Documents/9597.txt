taste, cultures and librarians' a position paper.
    contribution to an issue devoted to popular culture and
    libraries. introductory paper, contending that the messages
    which users receive from the products of their culture contain
    information which aids in the sense-making process. individuals
    can choose their own cultural boundaries rather than have those
    boundaries imposed upon them. whilst librarians may conclude
    that they do not wish to collect popular culture materials,
    they cannot hope to make correct decisions unless they
    understand the ramifications of choosing to exclude a series of
    information resources of substantive content, that are
    transferred by means of media unfamiliar to most
    libraries.
